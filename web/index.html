sanoTTS

सानो — small
a tiny neural text-to-speech voice · sano (सानो) is Nepali for small

A full neural TTS in ~1.4M parameters. No cloud, no NPU. Real-time on a $3 microcontroller — or right here in your browser.

duration → acoustic → iSTFT decoder, distilled from a Piper/VITS teacher and quantized to int8. Below: hear all four voices, and run the English runtime live on this page.

Run the runtime live

This synthesizes a fixed English sentence with the identical bit-exact C99 runtime that runs on the ESP32 — compiled to WebAssembly, executed client-side. No server, no network after load. It verifies its own output against the PyTorch reference in your browser.

Expanding

Three more teachers were trained end-to-end (~1.5M each), but the full student stack is not yet demo-quality — the acoustic/decoder lanes hold up in isolation, the composed stack doesn't (full-stack cosine vs teacher; higher is closer). Listening verdict below is candid, and there's no audio to ship yet.

Indonesiancosine 0.43 should be better at ~1.5M — in progress
Vietnamesecosine 0.53 should be better at ~1.5M — in progress
Telugucosine 0.56 full-student speech not yet usable — needs the interface fixed

The four above clear this bar comfortably. These three are a preview of the porting pipeline, not a quality claim — audio lands here when they cross into intelligible.

Open source under GPL-3.0 — builds on piper and espeak-ng. Distill your own tiny voice: github.com/Ampixa/sanoTTS.
