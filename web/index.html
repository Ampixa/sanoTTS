saanoTTS 🔊

sano (सानो) — Nepali for “small.” A tiny neural text-to-speech voice.

A full neural stack — duration → acoustic → iSTFT decoder — distilled from a Piper/VITS teacher down to ~1.4M parameters, small enough to run with no cloud and no NPU: real-time on a ~$3 ESP32-S3 microcontroller, or right here in your browser. Below you can hear all four voices and run the English runtime live on this page.

Hear the voices

One frozen evaluation sentence per language, three lanes each: saanoTTS is our full ~1.4M student stack, Piper teacher is the ~15M model it was distilled from (the reference), and oracle decoder feeds the teacher’s own acoustics through our small decoder — an upper bound on what the decoder can do. Trust your ear, not a metric.
